Chocolate Lava Cake

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
25 mins
Medium

Ingredients

  • 6 oz high-quality dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1/2 cup confectioners' sugar
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon espresso powder (optional, enhances chocolate flavor)
  • Vanilla ice cream, for serving
  • Fresh berries, for serving
  • Mint leaves, for garnish
  • Additional confectioners' sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425¬∞F (220¬∞C). Place a baking sheet in the oven while it preheats.
  2. Thoroughly butter four 6-ounce ramekins. Dust with flour and tap out excess. Place the prepared ramekins on a baking sheet.
  3. In a medium microwave-safe bowl, combine the chopped chocolate and butter. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until melted and smooth. Alternatively, use a double boiler.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, confectioners' sugar, and salt.
  5. In another bowl, whisk together eggs, egg yolks, vanilla extract, and espresso powder (if using) until well combined.
  6. Add the egg mixture to the melted chocolate mixture and whisk until fully incorporated.
  7. Gradually add the flour mixture to the chocolate mixture, gently folding with a spatula until just combined. Do not overmix.
  8. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins, filling each about 3/4 full.
  9. Place the ramekins on the preheated baking sheet and bake for 12-14 minutes, until the edges are firm but the centers are still soft. The tops should be set but slightly jiggly in the center.
  10. Remove from the oven and let stand for 1 minute.
  11. Run a knife around the edges to loosen. Place a dessert plate upside down on top of each ramekin, then carefully invert to release the cake.
  12. Dust with confectioners' sugar, garnish with mint leaves, and serve immediately with vanilla ice cream and fresh berries.

Apple Crumble

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
1 hr
Easy

Ingredients

  • For the Apple Filling:
  • 6 large apples (Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or a mix), peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • For the Crumble Topping:
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (walnuts or pecans, optional)
  • For Serving:
  • Vanilla ice cream
  • Caramel sauce (optional)
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish or a 10-inch cast iron skillet.
  2. Prepare the apple filling: In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice.
  3. In a separate small bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons brown sugar, 1 1/2 teaspoons cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Sprinkle this sugar-spice mixture over the apples and toss until evenly coated.
  5. Add the flour and vanilla extract to the apples and toss again until combined.
  6. Transfer the apple mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  7. Prepare the crumble topping: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, rolled oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt.
  8. Add the cold cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter, two forks, or your fingertips, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces of butter remaining.
  9. If using, stir in the chopped nuts.
  10. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the apple filling, covering the entire surface.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apple filling is bubbling around the edges.
  12. If the topping is browning too quickly, loosely cover with aluminum foil.
  13. Remove from the oven and let cool for 15 minutes to allow the filling to set slightly.
  14. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, a drizzle of caramel sauce if desired, and garnish with fresh mint leaves.

Strawberry Shortcake

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
45 mins
Medium

Ingredients

  • For the Biscuits:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 large egg
  • 2/3 cup cold heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sugar, for sprinkling (optional)
  • For the Strawberries:
  • 2 pounds fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • For the Whipped Cream:
  • 2 cups cold heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Mint leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the strawberries: In a large bowl, combine sliced strawberries, sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla. Gently stir to coat. Let sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the strawberries to release their juices.
  2. Make the biscuits: Preheat oven to 425¬∞F (220¬∞C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  4. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces of butter remaining.
  5. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the egg, heavy cream, and vanilla extract.
  6. Pour the wet ingredients into the flour mixture and stir just until the dough comes together. Do not overmix.
  7. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat into a 3/4-inch thick rectangle.
  8. Using a floured 2 1/2-inch round biscuit cutter, cut out biscuits and transfer to the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Gather scraps, gently pat together, and cut additional biscuits.
  9. Brush the tops of the biscuits with a little heavy cream and sprinkle with coarse sugar if desired.
  10. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown. Transfer to a wire rack to cool.
  11. Make the whipped cream: In a large bowl, combine the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Using an electric mixer, beat on medium-high speed until soft peaks form, about 3-4 minutes. Do not overbeat.
  12. Assemble the shortcakes: When ready to serve, split each biscuit horizontally with a serrated knife.
  13. Place the bottom half of each biscuit on a serving plate. Top with a generous spoonful of macerated strawberries and their juice.
  14. Add a dollop of whipped cream, then place the top half of the biscuit on the whipped cream.
  15. Top with another dollop of whipped cream and more strawberries.
  16. Garnish with mint leaves if desired and serve immediately.

Tiramisu

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
4 hrs
Medium

Ingredients

  • For the Coffee Mixture:
  • 1 1/2 cups strong brewed espresso or coffee, at room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons Marsala wine (or dark rum)
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (such as Kahl√∫a)
  • For the Cream Filling:
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 16 oz (2 cups) mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • For Assembly:
  • 24-30 Italian ladyfingers (Savoiardi)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 oz dark chocolate, finely grated
  • Fresh berries, for garnish (optional)
  • Mint leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the coffee mixture: In a shallow bowl, combine espresso, 2 tablespoons sugar, Marsala wine, and coffee liqueur. Stir until sugar dissolves. Set aside to cool completely.
  2. Make the cream filling: In a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water (double boiler), whisk together egg yolks and 3/4 cup sugar. Make sure the bottom of the bowl doesn't touch the water.
  3. Continue whisking constantly for about 8-10 minutes, until the mixture is pale yellow and has thickened. When you lift the whisk, the mixture should form ribbons that hold their shape for a few seconds.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. In a large bowl, whisk mascarpone cheese until smooth. Add the cooled egg yolk mixture, salt, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined and smooth.
  6. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  7. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture using a spatula, maintaining as much air as possible. Refrigerate until ready to use.
  8. Assemble the tiramisu: Select a 9x13-inch baking dish or a similar sized serving dish.
  9. Working one at a time, quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture (about 1 second per side - don't soak them or they'll fall apart). Arrange them in a single layer on the bottom of the dish, breaking some if necessary to fit.
  10. Spread half of the mascarpone cream mixture evenly over the ladyfingers.
  11. Dust lightly with half of the cocoa powder using a fine-mesh sieve.
  12. Create a second layer by repeating the process with the remaining ladyfingers dipped in coffee mixture.
  13. Top with the remaining mascarpone cream, spreading it evenly.
  14. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  15. Before serving, dust the top with the remaining cocoa powder and sprinkle with grated dark chocolate.
  16. Garnish with fresh berries and mint leaves if desired.
  17. Cut into squares and serve chilled.

Classic New York Cheesecake

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
6 hrs
Medium

Ingredients

  • For the Graham Cracker Crust:
  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs (about 15-16 full sheets)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • For the Cheesecake Filling:
  • 32 oz (4 packages) cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup sour cream, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • 5 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • For the Topping (Optional):
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • For Serving (Optional):
  • Fresh berries
  • Berry compote
  • Caramel sauce
  • Chocolate sauce
  • Mint leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the pan: Preheat oven to 325¬∞F (165¬∞C). Grease a 9-inch springform pan and wrap the outside with a double layer of heavy-duty aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in during baking.
  2. Make the crust: In a medium bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, melted butter, and salt. Mix until the crumbs are evenly moistened.
  3. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom and about 1-1.5 inches up the sides of the prepared springform pan. Use the bottom of a measuring cup to compact the crust.
  4. Bake the crust for 10 minutes. Remove from oven and let cool while preparing the filling. Keep the oven on.
  5. Make the filling: In a large bowl using a hand mixer or in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese on medium-low speed until smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes.
  6. Add the sugar and beat until well incorporated and smooth, about 2 more minutes, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  7. Add the sour cream, vanilla extract, and almond extract (if using). Beat until combined.
  8. Add the eggs one at a time, beating on low speed after each addition just until combined. Do not overmix.
  9. Add the flour, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Beat on low speed just until combined and smooth.
  10. Pour the cheesecake filling into the cooled crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
  11. Prepare water bath and bake: Place the foil-wrapped springform pan in a larger roasting pan. Pour hot water into the roasting pan until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the springform pan.
  12. Carefully transfer to the oven and bake for 55-70 minutes, or until the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle when the pan is gently shaken.
  13. Turn off the oven, crack the door open slightly, and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour. This gradual cooling helps prevent cracks.
  14. Optional sour cream topping: If using, mix together sour cream, sugar, and vanilla extract. Spread over the warm cheesecake, then return to the oven (still off but warm) for 10 minutes.
  15. Remove the cheesecake from the water bath and unwrap the foil. Let cool at room temperature for 1 hour.
  16. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  17. Serve: Run a thin knife around the edge of the pan before removing the springform ring. Transfer the cheesecake to a serving plate.
  18. Slice with a clean, warm knife (dip in hot water and wipe dry between cuts) and serve with optional toppings of your choice.

Nutrition Information

Calories: 490 | Protein: 7g | Carbs: 35g | Fat: 36g

Chocolate Chip Cookies

‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
30 mins
Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the creamed mixture, mixing well.
  6. Stir in chocolate chips.
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets.
  8. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until edges are lightly browned.
  9. Remove from oven and let cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes before transferring to wire racks to cool completely.
